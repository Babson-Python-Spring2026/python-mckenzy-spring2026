The rain began the same day the letter arrived.
It was stamped from a town no one in my family had ever mentioned.
Inside was a single sentence: “Come before the lighthouse goes dark.”
I thought it was a prank—until the power flickered.
By midnight, the entire coast had lost electricity except for one thin beam in the distance.
The lighthouse.
Against my better judgment, I drove through the storm toward the cliffside road.
Waves slammed against the rocks like they were trying to climb.
When I reached the top, the door was already open.
A spiral staircase led upward, glowing faintly gold.
At the lantern room, I found no keeper—only a mirror.
And in it, I saw myself… older, whispering, “You’re just in time.”
